Notes on why I chose JUCE over Qt:
1) First and the most important reason is that JUCE is a framework that was created for the purpose of real-time audio processing, while Qt is for more general development.
2) I have been looking into JUCE for about a year now so I am slightly familiar with how the framework is set up.
3) JUCE's sliders, spin-boxes and other features are similar to Qt's respective features. So, my understanding of Qt translates rather well to JUCE.
